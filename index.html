THE Human Origin Story

Am I created by a higher intelligence or by a random cosmic event? This is the question that tends to draw a divide between many religions and between scientists. There are many theories, beliefs and speculations that have sought to answer this existensial question. There can be parallels found between these explanations, but two things are for sure; our lives had a beginning, pointing to the indisputable fact of an end and within the many explanations of our origin, there exists one which holds the truth.

The scientific method seeks to answer the How, the What, the When, by and from Who, by Which way, the reason Why, and Where? Scientists when developing a theory have to come up with an undisputable and somehow replicatable answer to these questions. The conventional and well known process after identifying a problem is to do a search on other theories and come up with an initial hypothesis, in simple terms, a superficial theory of what the solution seems to be, or in a more simpler word - a guess. An experiment is then carried out to test this hypothesis. This is a repeating process which continues to build on itself until a presentable answer is found.

Limitations

Despite being full proof for many scientific problems, there are just some topics that cannot be proven or disproven by the scientific methodbecause they are beyond and above us. One of these topics being the human origin story.

Yet, there are currently 4 major scientific theories that attempt in explaining the human origin


Quantum Gravity Theories

String Theory 🔗 and Loop Quantum Gravity 🔗 are grouped into this we call Quantum Gravity Theory. QGT aims to reconcile General Relativity (which simply tries to explain the macro-level structures of the universe) with Quantum Mechanics (simply describing the micro-level structures of the universe)

This theory is itself theoretical as they are still being formulated. These theories lack testable predictions that can be verified with current technology and due to its extreme mathematical complexity, they are hard to work with and can sometimes predict phenomena which are beyond our ability to observer. There is no evidence to this effect.



The Big Bang Theory

Amongst some in the scientific community, the Big Bang is the most widely accepted explanation for the origin of the universe out of the other three. It posits that the universe began from a singularity - an extremely hot point with unimaginable pressure which had a sudden release of energy.

Evidence provided:

  • Cosmic Microwave Background Radiation

    • The above is what is called a fossil radiation. Discovered by Arno Penzias and Robert Wilson in 1965,this is basically the image produced from the most advanced telescope showing the remnants of light from the farthest parts of the universe, which cosmologists can use to get a glimpse into what it looked just 380,000 years after the big bang. Propenents of this theory propose that due to the uniformity of light as seen in the image, the theory of a single-dense point of light is supported.

Although well presented this evidence is, there are some pitfalls

Pitfalls:

  • Singularity Problem

    • The big bang proposes a point of infinite density and temperature called the singularity. This is a violation of the laws of physics as we know them and to assume this means that the proponents of this theory do believe there could exist elements of the cosmos which could break the laws of physics and it is just a matter of discovery. Many laws of physics have been broken before and continue to be.

  • The Horizon Problem

    • The universe today appears to have uniformity in temperature and density in areas that are distant. These areas, if considering just the Big Bang theory and not the Inflation theory, should not have similar temperatures as they would never have been in contact with each other.

  • Monopole Problem

    • The Big Bang theorizes that there are magnetic monopoles 🔗 (hypothetical particles with a single magnetic pole, imagine magnets with just south poles). But they are just hypothetical as of now and this does remove credibility from the theory.


Inflation Theory

As a fix to some of the problems that invalidated the Big Bang theory, Alan Guth in 1980 posited the Inflation Theory which states that a second after the Big Bang, the universe started expanding.

Evidence provided:

  • Hubble's Law

    • Observations by Edwin Hubble in the 1920s showed that galaxies are moving away from us, with more distant galaxies receding faster. This seems suggests that the universe is expanding and that there once was point of origin from which this expansion began.

  • Horizon problem Fix

    • A constantly expanding universe seems to explain why the there are uniformities in temperature and density between vast distances as they once were in contact before inflation pushed them apart.

  • Flatness Fix

    • Though not mentioned earlier, the Big Bang did not seem to explain the current scientific observation of the universe, not the earth, having a flat geometry. This is posited by the Inflation theory.

  • Primordial Fluctuations

    • As a new addition to the fixes of the Big Bang theory, this theory posited an explanation for why and how matter came together after the Big Bang to form galaxies, solar systems and planets.

      It posits that there were quantum fluctuations which caused the formation of galaxies.

With important fixes to the Big Bang theory, this theory seems infallible, however there is a down side to this

Pitfalls:

  • Lack of Direct Evidence

    • The evidence provided by the Cosmic Microwave Background (CMB) is an indirect evidence and is limited by humanity's latest technological level as there is a probability light patterns beyond the current reach of our most advanced telescope do show non-uniformity. Yet if we are to take the CMB as credible, it is an indirect evidence. So far there has been no direct evidence to proof the expansion of the universe whether through tests or elemental samples.

  • Fine Tuning

    • As with many theories, there are certain conditions and parameters that are initialized. Lets say for example, in measuring the distance from Santa Clarita to San Diego along the Freeway, we must initialize Santa Clarita as the starting point of the freeway, but the question is, what exact point in Santa Clarita are we initializing? Is it from the middle of the city or from boundaries of the city?

      This same issue is apparent in the development of the Inflation theory. Some of the conditions and parameters established as basis of this theory might not have been true at the beginning of time.

  • Multiversal Problem

    • Some versions of this theory imply the existence of a multiverse, which as of now is science fiction. This does remove credibility.



Multiverse Theory

This theory proposes that our universe is just one of many universes, possibly with different physical laws and constants. This theory is a product of the current means to provide evidence of Quantum Gravity Theories.

As with the QGT, this theory remains to be proven as the Multiverse is science fiction.
